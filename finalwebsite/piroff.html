Nevertheless, the risks are always greater than the opportunities. The main opportunities are:

  • Piracy is illegal: It is a federal crime to make, sell or download unlicensed material. Therefore, people who are found guilty of the crime can be fined up to $250,000, and/or sentenced to up 5 years in prison.
  • It is risky: Pirated software does not always contain the content itself. There is a huge chance that the pirated software is more likely to be infected with serious computer viruses, which can damage the user's computer system. Pirating isn't always safe, and the internet will always be the internet, in a sense of that the internet is never safe.
  • It is unproductive: Some software will come with manuals and paper work to help the user with the software and most pirated software does not come with manuals or technical support that is given to legitimate users. Thus, pirates are less likely to derive as much use out of software as users who have obtained the software through legal channels. In addition, software becomes outdated rapidly pirated software is ineligible for upgrades.

Software Piracy

Software piracy is any attempt to break the licence terms of a piece of software. This includes downloading and using a program without paying for it, as well as buying, selling or giving away illegitimate copies of a game or any other piece of software. It could mean extracting code from a program, or modifying it without permission in order to do something that the developers did not wish you to do.

The Moral Issues

It is ethically wrong to participate in piracy, as it significantly impacts the maker of the material heavily. It is reduces their income greatly and is illegal. It is a federal crime to make unauthorised copies of content and people who are found guilty of this crime can be fined up to $250,000, and/or sentenced up to 5 years in prison. Despite this, people still engage in piracy, the main reason of this is because the content is free! When people hear that games or software can be accessed for free but all you have to do is a bit of tinkering, the outcome is positive. Therefore, piracy is a big issue in the modern world.

Opportunites of Piracy

There are certain advantages to pirating software. Main benefits include:

  • Cost: pirated software costs nothing, or close to nothing, to obtain. With some consumer software packages now costing upwards of $500, the amount of money saved by pirating software is certainly not negligible.
  • Easy to Acquire: Pirated software is extremely easy to find, whether online, or sold openly in foreign countries, or simply provided by friends. In fact, in many cases, it easier for someone to go online and download an illegal copy of a software program than to drive to a store and buy it from a retailer.
  • Anonymity: Pirating software is a crime that can be committed without leaving one's house, or even interacting with any person. Thus, it is very tempting to commit this "victimless" crime.
  • Profit: Although most software pirates do not profit directly from pirating software, the temptation to make money by selling illegal programs is strong. All one needs to become a software pirate is a CD burner, which now cost as little as $100, and the pirate's computer can become a virtual cash cow.

What can we do against piracy?

There are many things that we can do against piracy, this can include:

  • Don't buy unlicensed copies: Buy software from an authorized dealer and don't use any program that is cleary unauthorised. When purchasing software, be sure to get the genuine product, manuals, and license documentation. Do some research on the price you can expect to get. If the product is distincly cheaper than the original then it is most likely pirated. It is also important to carefully read the license agreement that comes with the software, since license agreements differ from publisher to publisher. Don't buy software labeled 'Not for Resale.' Never purchase software labeled 'For Bundles Only' or 'OEM Distribution' without the appropriate hardware.
  • Keep everything that comes with the product: Keep the original receipt and invoice of the product, which are the proof that you have purchased legal software. Furthermore, always register your product so you can get all the technical support and possible upgrade you might need in the future.
  • Don't participate in any pirating Don't make copies unless it is allowed by the license agreement. Don't rent, lease, sublicense or lend the software or documentation to anyone. Don't download illegal software from warez sites or ftp servers.
  • Spread awareness: When you find out a friend or someone might be taking part in pirating, you should make them aware of the risks and that what they are doing is very illegal.

Offensive Communications

The Problem

The rise of the internet and social media in recent decades has fundamentally reshaped the way we engage with each other and as a society. This radical shift has brought many benefits, but there are also associated risks and harms, and it has proved challenging for the law to keep pace with this rapidly changing environment.
