General Information

As the world is constantly evolving, technology is forever changing and we are presented with the moral and ethical issues that concern every individual that is of use of technology. The number of people that are exposed to computers is growing, and the younger generations are wide open to various machines. The area of computer technology is one that is difficult to assign a specific set of moral codes, although it is necessary that ethics be considered when making decisions in this area. Computing creates a whole new set of ethical problems, unique for itself.


Points that we need to consider:
  • Security of data
  • Ensuring public safety
  • Computer misuse
  • Piracy
  • The environment

Security of data

Now that the technological world is growing, data is highly sought after. Companies can use the data they gather, whether it is behavioural or factual, to market products or services to people that they collect data from, which ultimately benefits companies.

Ensuring public safety

Public safety can be tied to data security, but a majority of this subject is the act of cyber-bullying and hacking. Anywhere in the world, safety is at risk and whoever is of use of a computer needs to take precaution.

Computer misuse

The misuse of computers is an illegal act and many people are in danger to this. This can include computer hacking, uploading malware or phishing. The list is long, but we need to be informed in how to recognise the misuse of computers and how to counter it.

The world is everchanging and this means that Earth is at risk of global warming. Due to this, e-waste needs to be reduces to the minimum to protect and sustain Earth.
